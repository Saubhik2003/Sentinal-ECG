About Sentinel

Our Mission

Sentinel was created to solve one of healthcare's most critical challenges: delayed diagnosis and emergency response. Every year, thousands of lives are lost due to late detection of medical emergencies. Our AI-powered platform acts as a digital health guardian, providing 24/7 monitoring and instant alerts to medical professionals.

The Problem We Solve

Traditional healthcare systems are reactive - patients visit doctors after symptoms appear. By then, conditions may have already progressed. Sentinel takes a proactive approach, using artificial intelligence to detect subtle patterns in health data that humans might miss, enabling early intervention and potentially life-saving alerts.

AI-Driven Healthcare

Our platform leverages state-of-the-art machine learning models trained on extensive medical datasets. These models analyze ECG patterns, stress indicators, and sleep disorders to generate a unified health risk score. When this score crosses critical thresholds, our system automatically initiates emergency protocols, notifying nurses, doctors, and emergency services.

Security & Data Protection

We take data privacy seriously. All patient data is encrypted end-to-end using industry-standard protocols. Patients maintain complete control over their data through our consent management system. Medical professionals can only access patient information with explicit permission. Our infrastructure is GDPR-compliant and follows HIPAA guidelines for medical data protection.

  • ✓ Database: MongoDB with encrypted collections

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Sentinel detect emergencies?

Sentinel uses advanced AI algorithms to continuously analyze your vital signs. When anomalies are detected or your health risk score enters the critical zone (61-100), the system automatically alerts our medical team and can dispatch emergency services if needed.

Is my health data secure?

Yes. All data is encrypted end-to-end and stored in HIPAA-compliant servers. You control who can access your information through our consent management system. Medical professionals can only view your data with your explicit permission.

How accurate is the AI prediction?

Our AI models are trained on extensive medical datasets and have been validated against clinical standards. However, AI predictions should be considered as assistive tools for medical professionals, not replacements for human clinical judgment.
